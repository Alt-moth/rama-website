Spousal Reunification and Integration Laws in Europe

Summary

Previously a world citizen, Rama Srinivasan is now learning to be a proud Bavarian and self-deprecating German. She trained as a journalist and travelled across India before deciding to explore the world, first with a journalistic stint in Oman and, later, with a Master’s degree in Boston. She completed her PhD in Anthropology at Brown University before moving again – this time as a trailing spouse – to Germany. After spending a beautiful but uncertain time as a Marie-Curie Postdoctoral Fellow at Ca' Foscari University of Venice in the middle of a global pandemic, she decided to permanently settle in rural Bavaria.

She has been writing and publishing since 2004 and, in January 2020, she published her first monograph, Courting Desire: Litigating for Love in North India, under the Rutgers University Press Imprint. She regularly publishes in newspapers and digital media and is currently working on two book projects.

An experienced qualitative researcher, Dr Srinivasan is a firm believer in anthropology's ability to provide solutions to key problems facing the world today through representative data collection, systemic analysis, and the recognised ability in synthesising vast amounts of information into texts of varying lengths and diverse purposes.
